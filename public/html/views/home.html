Our Story

"It's the unexpected that changes our lives"

How we met: Dustin and I met at our mutual friends' wedding in Katy, Texas. I had actually tried for weeks to find a wedding date, but it just didn't seem to work out for me. I think Matt and Stephanie had intentionally paired us up to walk down the aisle together, but they couldn't predict the rest. I ended up catching the bouquet (Stephanie has good aim) and Dustin caught the garter - looking back, it's like it was meant to be. We had a lot of fun getting to know each other the rest of the night, cheesy dancing and all. Dustin messaged me the next day and we continued to talk. A week after we met, I said, "Would it be crazy if I bought a plane ticket to Florida?" He said no, and two weeks later I was on a plane headed east. Neither of us expected to meet one another, let alone fall in love. I had never considered living in Florida, or even leaving Austin for that matter.

I really had no idea what to expect out of this growing relationship. After my visit, we continued to talk via Skype and Dustin came to visit for New Year's Eve. Stephanie and Matt also joined us, which made the whole weekend very fun and special. Up until this point, our relationship had no official title. This was the point in which we had to figure out if we wanted to be serious and move forward or go our different directions.

With it being so early in the relationship, it would have been easy to say goodbye and continue to live our lives in separate states. There were a few things that helped me make my decision to move forward with our relationship. The first being that Dustin is such a gentleman, which every lady can agree is hard to come by these days. It felt great to be treated the way a woman should be, especially when I hadn't been before. And the second deciding factor being that I could see myself having a loving family and happy life with him (his good looks helped a little, too!). We made our relationship official on January 1, 2015.

I made plans to visit him for Valentine's Day, and we had a romantic Valentine's dinner at Restaurant Paradis in 30A, where his long time friend Shane was our Chef that evening. This trip was also when I first met his family. I have to say, his family definitely gave him brownie points because they're an amazing family. I'm lucky to marry into it!

We knew that for our relationship to work, one of us would have to move. While Austin has and will always have my heart, I was ready for new career opportunities and Dustin had just started an amazing opportunity, so that's really what helped us make our decision. Plus, Florida has some amazing beaches! And it gave me the opportunity to get to know his amazing (and amazingly huge) family.

Moving to Florida ended up being the best possible career opportunity for both of us. The job I started upon moving here did not work out, but an opportunity that was so much better fell in my lap. We now both work for the same organization (Shout out to ITProTV!) that has provided us both with career growth and happiness. (I couldn't have made this website without them!) Looking back over the last two and half years, we've had our ups and our downs, but I am fully confident in our relationship and can't wait for what's to come.

The proposal: After a year and a half of being with Erin, I realized that she was someone I could really see spending the rest of my life with. I had intentions of marrying her to begin with, but it helps to spend time with someone at least for a little bit to really see if you jive together. Even though we are pretty different people and may not always see eye-to-eye, we have always managed to work with each other and get through issues. Anyone who knows her can vouch for her ferocity and loyalty, and loyalty is something I value a lot. She’s pretty smart, too, which is a huge plus. Being with her is kind of like having a pet tiger… with all of the benefits and dangers included. She will make a great mother. I call her my little cactus flower because she is really pretty to look at, but can be prickly when she wants to. I think she likes that, though. I think I do too--it makes things interesting.

At any rate, I started my scheming around July of 2016. Something in me sparked a fire, (no not Erin, ha) and I conjured up the nerve to call The Henderson Inn Bed and Breakfast in Destin, FL to book a room I thought would be romantic enough for us to stay in. I booked it for the weekend around October 24th, 2016 which marked our two year anniversary of meeting one another. Immediately, I started sweating profusely. I hadn’t even gotten the ring yet! Luckily, she already knew exactly what she wanted in a ring and never failed to remind me of what she picked out. So, that part was easy. Now I just needed to request her hand in marriage from her dad and scrounge up enough cash to get that diamond all the while keeping Erin in the dark. Throughout the next few months I did exactly that. Her dad gave us his blessing and I was ready to go. I conspired with my family to make it seem like we would be taking a “family beach vacation” around Oct. 24th. so I could get her to ask off without seeming too suspicious. I’m not exactly sure when she started to suspect, but eventually she did. It turns out she can be inconveniently smart when she wants to. We loaded up the car for a “beach trip” and took off down the road to Destin. Five hours later we roll up to The Henderson Inn. I turned to her, smiled, and said, “Yeah, so… we aren’t here for a family vacation.”

She gave me a look filled with smug amusement, “Yeah, I suspected as much.”

After that, I tried to play it off as just a “romantic getaway”--even as I had plans to propose that night at sunset. We got settled into our room, and took a stroll on the beach. Unbeknownst to me, there was a wedding going on that day. The event clashed with my plans, upsetting an otherwise great proposal opportunity. But, being the fly-by-the-seat-of-my-pants kind of guy I am, I chose at the last moment to do it the next night. I prayed for perfect weather and uninterrupted privacy, and by George I got it. We were able to take an uninterrupted stroll along the Henderson Park beach area. Sunset hit and I caught Erin watching it disappear beneath the horizon. I frantically wrote in the sand behind her: “Erin, will you marry me?” with one eye on her and one eye on the sand beneath me. Thankfully, she never turned around before I finished. With my heart racing, I walked up and embraced her from behind. I took the ring out of my pocket and held it in front of her. “Hey, look at what I found over here…”

The look she gave me I will never forget. Her eyes are really pretty--one of my favorite aspects of her beauty. But, the look in them that day was something really special. We walked over to the spot in the sand where I wrote my proposal. I got down on one knee as she read it, and asked, “Erin McCaffrey, will you marry me?” I presented her the ring, she said “Yes!” I slipped it on her finger and it was done. My life was over. Game over, bro. Game over.

Ceremony

Thursday, September 28, 2017 | 6:00PM

Lady Bird Johnson Wildflower Center

We chose the LBJ Wildflower Center after looking at a few venues in the Austin area. We were looking for a beautiful outdoor venue that captured the essence of the Hill Country. While we were looking at the venue, my mom told us that my grandmother actually sold some of her handpainted wildflower shirts in the gift shop years ago, and had the honor of meeting Lady Bird Johnson herself. We knew then that this was the perfect place to get married.

Directions to LBJ Wildflower Center

4801 La Crosse Ave., Austin, TX 78739

Please allow at least 45 minutes for travel to the ceremony. Traffic conditions may not be optimal.

Attire: Semiformal

The venue lies in the beautiful Hill Country with Hill Country inspired architecture and native gardens. The LBJ Wildflower Center uses native plants to restore and create healthy, beautiful landscapes. Their mission is to inspire the conservation of native plants through their gardens, research, education, consulting and outreach programs. In doing so, they improve water quality, provide habitat for wildlife and enhance human health and happiness.
